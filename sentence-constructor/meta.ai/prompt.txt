types of arabic, here is some info on those types:

            Modern Standard Arabic (MSA - الفصحى المعاصرة):

            Derived from Classical Arabic but simplified for contemporary use.
            Used in media, formal speeches, education, and official documents across the Arab world.
            It’s not the native tongue of any population but is a unifying language for Arabic speakers.
            Colloquial Arabic (العامية - Al-‘Ammiyah):

            Spoken dialects that vary greatly by region.
            Each country or even city may have its own dialect. The main groups include:
            Egyptian Arabic (المصرية) — widely understood due to Egypt’s strong cultural influence.
            Levantine Arabic (الشامية) — spoken in Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, and Palestine.
            Gulf Arabic (الخليجية) — spoken in countries like Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Qatar.
            Maghrebi Arabic (المغاربية) — used in North Africa (Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, etc.).
            Iraqi Arabic (العراقية) — spoken in Iraq.
            Mixed Arabic (اللغة الوسيطة - Al-Lugha Al-Wusta):

            A blend of MSA and local dialects, often used in TV shows, talk shows, and informal public speaking.
            It’s a middle-ground form of Arabic for cross-regional communication.
            Specialized Arabic:

            Business Arabic — uses MSA with specific vocabulary related to commerce and economics.
